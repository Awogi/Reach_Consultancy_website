Study In America

Studying in America offers an unparalleled educational experience, with access to prestigious institutions and diverse academic programs. The U.S. is home to some of the world's top universities, known for their cutting-edge research, high academic standards, and innovative teaching methods. Whether you want to study in a major metropolitan area or a peaceful college town, America provides a wide variety of environments for both academic and personal growth.

International students are welcomed with open arms, and they can engage in an enriching cultural experience. America offers a broad range of activities, from vibrant city life and entertainment to national parks and landmarks like the Grand Canyon and Statue of Liberty, making it an ideal place for students seeking both education and adventure.


Why Study in America?


World-Class Universities

The U.S. is home to some of the top-ranked universities globally, offering diverse and rigorous programs to cater to all academic interests.

Cultural Diversity

Students have the opportunity to interact with people from various backgrounds, broadening their perspectives and enhancing cross-cultural understanding.

Work Opportunities

International students can work part-time during their studies and full-time during breaks, gaining valuable work experience while supporting their finances.

Post-Study Work Opportunities

The U.S. offers programs like the Optional Practical Training (OPT), allowing graduates to gain work experience in their field for up to 12 months or longer in some cases.

Innovative Research & Technology

Studying in the U.S. provides access to advanced research facilities and technologies, opening doors for students to be part of groundbreaking innovations.

Requirements for Studying in America


Choose a Program & Institution

Explore the programs and universities that align with your academic and career goals.

English Proficiency

Ensure you meet the required English language proficiency, typically through tests like PTE or IELTS.

Standardized Tests

Some programs may require standardized test scores like GRE, SAT, or ACT, depending on the level and field of study.

Financial Evidence

Demonstrate your ability to support yourself financially while studying in the U.S., including tuition and living expenses.

Student Visa

Apply for an F-1 student visa and provide necessary documentation, such as your acceptance letter and financial proof.

Pre-Departure Preparations

Plan for accommodation, travel, health insurance, and orientation programs before you leave.

Benefits for Nepali Students


World-Class Education

Access to prestigious universities and academic programs that offer global recognition.

Research Opportunities

Engage in groundbreaking research and projects in a wide range of fields.

Post-Study Work Opportunities

Take advantage of OPT and other work programs to gain practical experience in the U.S. after graduation.

Scholarships & Financial Aid

Numerous scholarships and financial aid options are available for Nepali students to help reduce the cost of education in the U.S.
